Recent work

Cif Bond Analyzer

Contributed to some troubleshooting with this project by Bob Lee, a peer of mine at Columbia. CBA is an interactive, command-line Python application designed for high-throughput extraction of minimum bond length and atomic mixing information from CIF files, used in solid-state chemistry. Check out the repo here.

Hückel Theory

Used Hückel theory to explore isomeric dimers by analyzing frontier orbitals and their orbital energies. This approach simplifies electron behavior, assuming sigma and pi electrons can be separated, and helps discern differences in bonding and stability between isomers based on eigenvalue ordering. Check out more here.

Grad school

City University of New York, Hunter College

I'll be officially attending CUNY this Fall. I'm currently working with Dr. Anton Oliynyk on synthesizing and studying novel intermetallic compounds. We use high-temperature methods like sintering and arc-melting to create these compounds, and then analyze their crystal structures using techniques like powder and single crystal diffraction. Once synthesized, we use electronic structure calculations to understand the chemical bonding and machine-learning approaches to predict their crystal structures and physical properties. Our research focuses particularly on intermetallics involving uranium, thorium, and rare-earth elements, with every machine-learning model we develop being experimentally validated.
